Sampling

  • Sampling is used to convert CT signals to DT (discretizing)
  • TT (seconds/sample) = sampling interval
  • fsfs (samples/second) = sampling rate
  • How does sampling affect the information in the signal?

Sampling Loss

  • No information between samples is known
  • Information is distorted

In class Example:

  • Playing a song at increasingly lower sampling rates
  • Starting at fsfs = 44 kHz, ending at 344 Hz
  • Progressively sounds more and more distorted
  • Interesting note: base is not distorted as much as the rest of the sound
  • Higher frequencies alias down to lower frequencies, causing lower distortions

Efects of samplin

  • NyQuist frequency
  • If frequencies in the CT signal are greater than the ase bad, they alias to frequencies in the base band
  • Anti-aliasing can be used to prevent distortions
